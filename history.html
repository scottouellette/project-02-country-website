History

Indigenous Roots and Mining Era

The Wood River Valley was seasonally used by the Shoshone, Bannock, and Lemhi tribes. In the 1870s and 1880s, European settlers populated the area, transforming the nearby town of Ketchum into a booming silver and lead mining hub. By 1893, the mining boom had collapsed, and the valley transitioned into a sheep-ranching economy.

The Hemingway Legacy

Novelist Ernest Hemingway first visited Sun Valley in 1939, where he finished writing For Whom the Bell Tolls. He spent the later years of his life in nearby Ketchum and is buried in the Ketchum Cemetery

Corporate Hub

Today, the area is widely known for hosting the exclusive annual Allen & Company conference (often dubbed "summer camp for billionaires"), drawing top global business leaders and media moguls.
